About Oscar Wilde

Oscar Fingal O'Flahertie Wills Wilde (1854-1900) was an Irish poet and playwright. He became one of the most successful playwrights of the late Victorian era in London and one of the greatest celebrities of his day, known for his brilliant wit, flamboyant style, and infamous imprisonment for indecency.

His works include "The Picture of Dorian Gray," "The Importance of Being Earnest," and many celebrated essays and poems. His wit and wisdom continue to inspire and entertain readers around the world.
